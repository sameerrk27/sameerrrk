# Portfolio Building for Data Analysts

A strong portfolio is the **most effective way to get a data analyst job**, especially when you're early in your career.

---

## 🏗️ What Makes a Good Portfolio Project?

### Must-haves:
- ✅ Real dataset (not toy/tutorial data — use Kaggle, government data, web scraping)
- ✅ Clear business question or hypothesis
- ✅ End-to-end: data collection → cleaning → analysis → insights → visualization
- ✅ Documented findings that a non-technical person could understand
- ✅ Code that is clean, commented, and readable

### Nice-to-haves:
- ⭐ Interactive dashboard (Power BI, Tableau Public, Streamlit)
- ⭐ Clear README with findings and business recommendations
- ⭐ Domain context (know your industry)
- ⭐ Shows your thought process, not just the final result

## 📋 Portfolio Structure

Your portfolio should have:

1. **2-3 showcase projects** (deep, well-documented)
2. **3-5 smaller projects** (breadth of skills)
3. **Clear GitHub README** with project descriptions
4. **Links to dashboards** (Tableau Public, Power BI published)

---

## 📝 README Template

```markdown
# Project Name

## 🎯 Business Question
What problem are you solving? Who cares about this answer?

## 📊 Dataset
- Source: [link]
- Size: X rows × Y columns
- Time period: 2020-2024

## 🛠️ Tools Used
Python (Pandas, Matplotlib), SQL, Power BI

## 🔍 Key Findings
1. Finding 1 — business implication
2. Finding 2 — business implication
3. Finding 3 — business implication

## 💡 Recommendations
What should the business do based on your analysis?

## 🗂️ Files
- `analysis.ipynb` — Full Jupyter notebook
- `dashboard.pbix` — Power BI dashboard
- `data/` — Raw and cleaned datasets
```
