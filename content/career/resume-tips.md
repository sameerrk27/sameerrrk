# Data Analyst Resume Tips

Your resume has ~6 seconds to make an impression. Make every line count.

---

## 📋 Data Analyst Resume Structure

```
[Name]
[Email] | [LinkedIn] | [GitHub] | [Portfolio URL]
[City, State/Country]

SUMMARY (2-3 lines)
Results-driven Data Analyst with X years of experience in...

SKILLS
Languages: SQL (MySQL), Python (Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-learn)
BI Tools:  Power BI (DAX, Power Query), Tableau
Other:     Excel (Pivot Tables, VLOOKUP), Statistics, EDA

EXPERIENCE
Job Title | Company | Dates
• Bullet with METRIC
• Bullet with METRIC
• Bullet with METRIC

PROJECTS
Project Name | Tools Used
• What you did + impact

EDUCATION
Degree | Institution | Year

CERTIFICATIONS
```

---

## 🎯 Writing Strong Bullet Points

**Formula: Action verb + What you did + Impact/Result**

### Bad bullets:
- ❌ "Analyzed data"
- ❌ "Created dashboards"
- ❌ "Worked with SQL"

### Good bullets:
- ✅ "Analyzed 50,000+ customer records to identify churn patterns, reducing churn by 15%"
- ✅ "Built Power BI dashboard tracking $4.18M+ in net sales, replacing 5 manual Excel reports"
- ✅ "Optimized SQL queries using CTEs and window functions, reducing report runtime from 45s to 3s"

---

## 🔑 Keywords to Include

ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems) scan for keywords. Include:

**Technical:**
SQL, Python, Pandas, NumPy, Power BI, Tableau, Excel, DAX, Power Query,
Matplotlib, Seaborn, Scikit-learn, Data Cleaning, EDA, Statistical Analysis,
A/B Testing, Data Visualization, ETL, Machine Learning

**Soft Skills / Business:**
Data Storytelling, Stakeholder Communication, Business Intelligence,
KPI Tracking, Dashboard Development, Root Cause Analysis

---

## ⚡ Quick Wins

1. **One page** for <5 years experience
2. **Quantify everything** — "10%" better than "significant"
3. **List tools in skills section** — recruiters ctrl+F for "SQL"
4. **Tailor for each job** — match their keywords
5. **PDF format** — preserves formatting
6. **Portfolio link** — clickable and up-to-date
7. **No photo, no objective statement** (in most countries)
8. **Reverse chronological order** — most recent first
